# Section 3 — Actions / Follow-Ups Reference

Section 3 is a dated activity log of real actions taken. It shows what actually happened in response to the complaint — not what the guest said, not what the problem was.

Format for every entry: `[Date] — [What was done]`

---

## What TO include

Include anything that actually moved the case forward — real communications, real decisions, real outcomes. If something happened that affected the guest, the partner, or the resolution, it goes here.

- Partner contacted with a specific request
  - e.g. `27/05 — Partner contacted regarding reported cleanliness concerns and requested additional cleaning.`
- Follow-up communications with specific detail
  - e.g. `27/05 — Follow-up email sent with additional details about spiders in the entryway.`
- Guest photos received and forwarded to the partner
  - e.g. `27/05 — Guest provided photos of stained towel, damaged dresser, and unclean kitchen drawers. Photos forwarded to partner.`
- Compensation offered, accepted, or declined
  - e.g. `27/05 — Offered guest 15% future stay discount as goodwill gesture.`
  - e.g. `24/05 — Guest declined the 15% discount — escalated to team lead for review.`
- Calls made with outcome noted
  - e.g. `14/05 — Called front desk — confirmed guest was correctly assigned to an ocean-view room.`
- Escalations — to TL, partner, or Airbnb
  - e.g. `27/05 — Guest escalated to Airbnb requesting 50% partial refund. Partner updated and asked to provide additional information.`
- Partner confirmed repairs or actions taken on-site
  - e.g. `05/16 — Partner confirmed on-site team attended to reported issues and concerns resolved.`
- Followed up with guest after a resolution attempt
  - e.g. `27/05 — Followed up with guest to confirm resolution on their side.`
- Refunds or compensation processed
  - e.g. `21/05 — Refund of €25 processed. Guest confirmed receipt.`
- Case escalated internally to management
  - e.g. `26/05 — Case escalated to management team for refund/compensation decision; guest informed accordingly.`
- Substantive status update sent to guest
  - e.g. `28/05 — Guest advised team is still awaiting response from on-site team.`

---

## What NOT to include

These are admin tasks and status notes — they clutter the section and add no useful information about what actually happened.

- "Acknowledged guest complaint" or "Guest concerns acknowledged" — acknowledging a complaint is not an action. Skip it.
- "HubSpot ticket created and documented" — this is an admin step. It doesn't tell anyone what you actually did.
- "Photos received and uploaded to HubSpot ticket" — uploading to HubSpot is filing, not an action. If photos were forwarded to the partner, note that instead.
- "Suggested compensation amount of €55 added" — this is a ticket field, not an action you took.
- "Awaiting partner response" or "Pending partner response" — these are status notes. They belong in the ticket status, not here.
- "Guest advised matter is being investigated" — vague and adds nothing. Note the actual communication sent instead.
- "Partner contacted and awaiting response" — incomplete. Say what you contacted the partner about and what you requested.
- "Guest reported unresolved concerns; photos reviewed internally" — reviewing photos internally belongs in Section 2 (Verification), not here.
- "Guest advised they booked alternate accommodation" — a guest's action is not your action. Only note it if it changes what you then did.
- The initial complaint or any restatement of the issues — that belongs in Section 1. Never open Section 3 with an entry like "Guest complained about X."
- What the partner said in response — partner responses belong in Section 2 (Verification).

---

## Key rules

- Start from the first real action taken — not from when the complaint arrived
- Every entry must be dated
- Be specific — say what was requested, what was offered, what was decided
- If no follow-ups have been taken yet, write: `No follow-ups yet.`
