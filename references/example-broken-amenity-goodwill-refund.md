Real case used as a reference for how to handle a broken shared amenity complaint where the guest booked specifically for that facility, and where Leavetown issued a goodwill refund without a formal partner response.

- Guest: [Guest]
- Booking ID: [Booking ID]

## Ticket body

1. Complaint Overview
The guest booked this property specifically for its wellness facilities (hammam and sauna). Both were completely out of service due to a breakdown throughout the entire 2-night stay. The guest raised the issue directly with on-site staff during the stay but received no resolution. They contacted us requesting a discount or refund.

2. Verification
Guest confirmed the issue was raised with on-site staff during the stay. No formal partner response was received regarding the cause or expected duration of the closure.

3. Follow-Ups / Actions Taken
18 May 2026 — Team asked guest to confirm whether the closure was temporary or permanent, and whether on-site staff had been informed.
21 May 2026 — Guest advised that team is still awaiting a response; update to follow.
28 May 2026 — EUR 50 goodwill refund processed via Booking.com. 15% discount on next stay offered.

4. Resolution
Pending — case ongoing.

---

## Notes

- The Booking.com listing described the unit as including a sauna — in reality this is a shared paid campsite facility (EUR 10/person for 2h). The complaint was categorised as Property Condition (broken amenity) rather than Content & Listing Accuracy because the core issue was the outage, not a listing misrepresentation.
- No photos were submitted by the guest.
- No formal partner response was received — Section 2 notes this clearly rather than leaving it blank.
- Section 3 starts from the first team action taken, not from the guest's complaint.
