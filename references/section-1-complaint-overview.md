# Section 1 — Complaint Overview Reference

Section 1 is a full snapshot of the situation. Anyone picking up this case — without reading the guest messages — must be able to understand exactly what happened, why, and what the guest wants.

---

## What to include

Write a brief 1–2 sentence summary of the issue reported by the guest, specifically describing the problem they experienced. Then cover:

- What happened (the core issue)
- Why it happened, if the guest gave a reason
- What the guest wants (refund, apology, explanation, etc.)
- Escalation notice, if applicable
- How the complaint evolved, if it changed across the thread

---

## Format rules

- Plain English only — no message quoting, no jargon
- Maximum 5 sentences or a short bullet list — never a wall of text
- Never repeat guest name, booking ID, channel, or stay status — the ticket header already has this. Just say "the guest."
- No asterisks or markdown formatting — HubSpot does not render it
- Section header written as: `1. Complaint Overview` (not bold, not underlined)
- Neutral point of view — do not take sides or editorialize

---

## Escalation flag rule

If escalation_flag is true, the very first line of this section must flag it clearly before anything else.

Examples:
- `⚠️ Early departure risk.`
- `⚠️ Guest has escalated to Airbnb.`
- `⚠️ Guest threatening to leave a negative review.`

---

## If the complaint evolved across the thread

Summarise the shift explicitly — do not just describe the final state.

Example:
`Initially raised as a cleanliness concern, escalated to a full refund demand when the partner did not respond within 24 hours.`

---

## Examples by complaint type

**Cleaning issue** — bullet point format works well, list specific items found:
- Sticky kitchen counters with crumbs and food stains
- Dirty dishes left inside the dishwasher
- Hair on the bathroom floor and inside the shower
- Unclean toilet
- Stained and unpleasant-smelling bedsheets
- Dust buildup on furniture and ceiling fans

**Broken amenity** — name the amenity and briefly describe the issue:
- Microwave not working — guest stated that when attempting to turn it on, nothing happens

**Noise complaint** — include what happened AND how it affected the guest so the severity is clear:
- Guest reported loud music and shouting coming from a nearby unit late at night
- Guest stated they were unable to sleep properly due to the noise
- Front desk was contacted but the noise continued afterward

**WiFi issue** — include the impact on the guest:
- Guest reported that the WiFi was not working throughout the stay
- Unable to connect multiple devices to the network
- Guest stated this impacted their ability to work remotely

**Water supply issue:**
- Guest advised there was no hot water in the unit
- Water pressure in the shower was extremely low
- Guest was unable to have a hot shower throughout entire stay

**Appliance issue:**
- Washing machine not functioning properly
- Guest stated the machine stops mid-cycle and clothing remains wet

**Parking issue:**
- Guest advised that the allocated parking bay was occupied upon arrival
- Unable to access the secure parking included with the booking
- Guest had to park elsewhere at an additional cost

**Power issue:**
- Guest experienced repeated power outages in the unit
- Certain lights and plugs were not working properly

**Check-in issue** — always state how long the guest had to wait:
- Guest experienced delays during the check-in process
- They were unable to enter for 2 hours (always state how long they had to wait)
- Unit was not ready upon arrival despite the confirmed check-in time

**Content / listing accuracy issue** — describe the specific amenity, feature, or item that was missing, incorrect, or different from what was advertised. Brief 1–2 sentences:
- Guest reported that the hot tub advertised on the listing was not available on site.
- Guest reported that bed linen was not provided despite being listed as included.
- Guest reported that the property did not have WiFi as advertised on the listing.
- Guest reported that the unit did not have a dishwasher as shown on the listing.

**Content/listing accuracy (evolved complaint):**
`The guest booked based on the listing description stating a private pool was included. On arrival, the pool was shared with the adjacent unit. Initially raised as a listing inaccuracy, the complaint escalated when the guest requested to be moved to another property and was told none were available. They are now requesting a full refund and have threatened to escalate to Airbnb.`

**Check-in access (with escalation flag):**
`⚠️ Early departure risk. The guest was unable to access the property at check-in — the smart lock code did not work and no on-site contact was reachable. After two hours, the guest advised they are considering leaving and booking elsewhere. They are requesting resolution within the hour or a full refund.`

---

## What NOT to include

- The guest's name — write "the guest" throughout
- The booking ID, channel, resort name, or stay status — these are already in the ticket header
- Direct quotes from the guest's messages
- Your own opinion on whether the complaint is valid
- Phrases like "the guest is unhappy" — describe what happened, not how they feel about it
- Internal notes like "ticket created following JetChat message"
